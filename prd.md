# Target Users and Personas

## Primary Users

### 1. Intake Specialist / Case Manager
**Role:** Responsible for capturing information from the consultation and coordinating follow-up.

**Typical responsibilities:**
- Record or upload consultation calls
- Review AI-generated summaries
- Request missing documents from clients
- Track document submissions
- Prepare the case package for attorney review

**Pain points today:**
- Manually writing notes after every consultation
- Chasing clients repeatedly for missing materials
- Tracking case status across email, documents, and case management tools

### 2. Attorney / Supervising Lawyer
**Role:** Responsible for evaluating the case and deciding the legal direction.

**Typical responsibilities:**
- Review fact summary and timeline
- Identify legal issues and next steps
- Decide whether to accept the case
- Request additional documents or clarification
- Draft or review legal documents

**Pain points today:**
- Inconsistent or incomplete case summaries from intake teams
- Time spent reconstructing facts from scattered notes and emails
- Lack of structured information before making case decisions

### 3. Firm Owner / Operations Lead
**Role:** Responsible for operational efficiency and business performance.

**Typical responsibilities:**
- Monitor intake efficiency
- Track conversion from consultation to signed case
- Manage staff workload and productivity
- Standardize intake procedures across staff

**Pain points today:**
- Intake process depends heavily on individual staff habits
- Difficult to measure follow-up speed and consistency
- Lost cases due to slow or inconsistent follow-ups

# Core User Workflows and Use Cases

## Use Case 1: Generate Structured Case Summary After Consultation
**User:** Intake specialist

**Current Workflow:**
1. Consultation call ends
2. Intake staff writes manual notes
3. Information is scattered across call recordings, notes, and emails

**Desired Outcome:**
A structured case summary is automatically generated after the call.

**System Capability:**
- Transcribe consultation call
- Extract structured facts (parties, timeline, events)
- Generate a case summary for internal review
- Allow intake staff to edit and confirm before attorney review

## Use Case 2: Track Missing Documents and Client Follow-ups
**User:** Intake specialist

**Current Workflow:**
1. Staff manually emails the client asking for documents
2. Documents arrive via email attachments
3. Staff tracks missing items manually

**Desired Outcome:**
The system automatically identifies required documents and tracks what has been submitted.

**System Capability:**
- Generate document checklist based on case type
- Show submitted vs missing materials
- Send follow-up requests to clients
- Track document submission status

## Use Case 3: Provide Attorneys With a Structured Case Review Package
**User:** Attorney

**Current Workflow:**
1. Attorneys receive inconsistent summaries from staff
2. Important facts may be missing or unclear
3. Attorneys must ask for clarification

**Desired Outcome:**
Attorneys receive a structured case review package.

**System Capability:**
- Generate a package including:
  - Clean fact pattern
  - Timeline of events
  - Key legal issues
  - Suggested next steps
  - Links to supporting documents

## Use Case 4: Convert Intake Data Into Draft Legal Documents
**User:** Attorney or paralegal

**Current Workflow:**
1. Staff manually re-enters information into legal documents
2. Significant duplication of work

**Desired Outcome:**
Structured intake data can be reused to generate draft documents.

**System Capability:**
- Generate editable drafts such as:
  - Affidavit
  - Declaration
  - Petition input packet
  - Internal case memo

## Use Case 5: Standardize Intake Workflows Across the Firm
**User:** Firm owner / operations lead

**Current Workflow:**
1. Intake process varies by staff member
2. Knowledge lives in individual experience

**Desired Outcome:**
The firm can define repeatable intake workflows.

**System Capability:**
- Define intake playbooks for specific case types
- Standardize follow-up steps and document requests
- Track workflow completion across cases
